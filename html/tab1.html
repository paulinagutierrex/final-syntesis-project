Galilean Relativity


What is it about?

Galilean relativity states that the same rules of physics apply in any frame of reference that is moving in a straight line at constant speed. basically, the laws of motion are the same in all inertial frames of reference. Galileo Galilei first described this principle in 1632 in his Dialogue Concerning the Two Chief World Systems using the example of a ship travelling at constant velocity, without rocking, on a smooth sea; any observer below the deck would not be able to tell whether the ship was moving or stationary.

Galilean Transformations

A Galileo transformation is a change of coordinates and velocities that leaves Newton's equations invariant. The previous condition is equivalent to the fact that the transformation between the coordinates of an inertial reference system and another inertial system that moves with respect to the first is also a Galileo transformation. Galileo transformations include the transformation equations of position and time, velocity and acceleration measured in two inertial time frames. As already stated, these equations allow us to establish whether a quantity is invariant when there is a change in coordinates.
